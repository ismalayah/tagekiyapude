Rocky Lombardi is the quarterback of the Northern Illinois Huskies football team representing NIU. 

Rocky Lombardi is the son of American football and coach Tom Lombardi. His father has been the defensive backs coach at the University of Wisconsin–Stout since 2016. In 1999, he was also the interim head football coach at Eastern Michigan University for one game.

Rocky Lombardi may not be a quarterback with impressive stats, but he had been improving every time. Chicago Suntimes penned down the QB’s opinion and his performance to date.

Who Is Rocky Lombardi?

Rocky Lombardi is the quarterback for Northern Illinois University. He is a prominent player on the football team.

He was officially introduced to college football in 2019. However, he had been playing football since a young age. His father is a player himself.

He played as a QB in Valley High School. He was awarded as Manning National Quarterback of the week at Michigan State University.

He was born on July 30, 1998, in Clive, IA, United States. There is an interesting story behind his first name.

He was named after Rocky Bleier, a former Pittsburgh Steelers standout that his father, Tony, liked. From 1968 through 1980, Bleier rushed for over 4,000 yards in parts of 11 seasons with the Steelers.  Thus, he is named after a mid-1970s sports hero from Pennsylvania.

Is Rocky Lombardi Related To Vince Lombardi?

Rocky Lombardi is not related to Vince Lombardi. Though their last name may be similar, they have no family relations.

In addition, the fact may not be relevant if it is about his relationship with the sports legend Vince Lombardi. But, if one looks into his family details, his great-grandfather has the same name.

So, in one way, he has the relation with the name Vince Lombardi but not with the sportsperson netiznes are curious about.

Rocky Lombardi Father Is Tony Lombardi

Tony Lombardi is the father of Rocky Lombardi. His father went to Rich East High School in Park Forest, Illinois. His father, Bob Lombardi, coached him to play football from an early age.

He used to play as a tailback on the football team during high school. He was a running back for the Arizona State University Sun Devils from 1980 to 1983, earning a varsity letter all four years.

However, after being undrafted in the NFL Draft in 1984, he signed as a free agent with the Chicago Bears. He did not, however, make the team’s roster.
